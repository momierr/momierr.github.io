I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Quantenbit AG team led by Prof. Dr. Ferdinand Schmidt-Kaler at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, Germany. Before that, I was a PhD student working at the Laboratoire Interdisciplinaire Carnot de Bourgogne (ICB, UMR CNRS 6303, Dijon, FR) and at the Institute for Physical Research of the National Academy of Sciences of Armenia (IPR-NAS, Ashtarak, AM), working under the supervision of Prof. Claude Leroy, Prof. Aram Papoyan and Dr. Armen Sargsyan. My research is focused on trapped-ion quantum computing and atomic physics. I also worked as a teaching assistant at Université de Bourgogne. I taught geometrical and wave optics (exercises, labs) and electromagnetism (labs) to 2nd year students (BSc. in Physics), and fluid mechanics/biophysics (labs) to 2nd year students in Biology. I have also been a reviewer for several journals including New Journal of Physics and Optics Letters.
