I am a PhD student working at the Laboratoire Interdisciplinaire Carnot de Bourgogne (ICB, UMR CNRS 6303, Dijon, FR) and at the Institute for Physical Research of the National Academy of Sciences of Armenia (IPR-NAS, Ashtarak, AM), working under the supervision of Prof. Claude Leroy, Prof. Aram Papoyan and Dr. Armen Sargsyan . My research is dedicated to atomic physics, especially the study of the behavior of alkali vapors confined in nanometric-thin cells in the presence of an external magnetic field. I also work as a teaching assistant at Université de Bourgogne. I teach geometrical and wave optics (exercises, labs) and electromagnetism (labs) to 2nd year students (BSc. in Physics), and fluid mechanics/biophysics (labs) to 2nd year students in Biology.
